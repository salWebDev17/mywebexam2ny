Chapter 45: The Awakening

The cavern was silent, save for the rhythmic dripping of water echoing off the obsidian walls. Kaelen held his breath, his hand gripping the hilt of his sword so tightly his knuckles turned white. He could feel the heat radiating from the massive shape slumbering ahead.

It was a dragon. Not the small, winged pests that plagued the northern farms, but an ancient beast, its scales the color of cooled magma. Smoke curled lazily from its nostrils, each exhale sending a wave of sulfurous air washing over the young knight.

"You shouldn't be here, little mouse," a voice rumbled. It didn't come from the dragon's mouth, but echoed directly inside Kaelen's mind. The sheer force of the telepathic voice dropped him to his knees.

He looked up, meeting a single, massive golden eye that had cracked open to stare at him. The pupil was a vertical slit of pure, ancient intelligence.

"I... I seek the Flame of Ancestry," Kaelen stammered, hating how small his voice sounded in the vast chamber. "The prophecy stated that only the dragon's fire could reforge the broken blade."

The dragon let out a sound that might have been a laugh, shaking the very foundations of the cave. Dust rained down from the ceiling. "Prophecies," the dragon mused. "Mortals and their desperate need for destiny. Tell me, Kaelen of Oakhaven, do you truly believe you are worthy?"

Before Kaelen could answer, the dragon shifted. The sound of scales scraping against stone was deafening. The beast lowered its massive head until it was inches from Kaelen's face. The heat was unbearable.

"Worthiness is not given by ancient scrolls," the dragon whispered, its golden eye burning into Kaelen's soul. "It is forged in the fires of sacrifice. Show me your sacrifice, little mouse, and I will show you the flame."
